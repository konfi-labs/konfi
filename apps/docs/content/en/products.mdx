# Products

Products define what the store and admin order forms can sell. Product data is
channel-scoped, so always check the active channel before creating or editing.

## Product Model

Treat a product as the reusable sellable configuration for one channel. A product
can also be exposed to other channels through links, but its source record still
lives under the channel where it was created.

- **Source channel** controls the product record, prices, media, SEO, and
  translations.
- **Linked channels** can find and use the product without copying it into a
  second catalog.
- **Customer links** mark a product as customer-specific. In order pricing,
  globally configured customer discounts are not applied to products linked to
  that customer.
- **Supplier and warehouse links** keep operational context close to the product
  for purchasing and fulfillment.
- **Storefront visibility** depends on active state, publication dates,
  expiration dates, price validity, translations, and cache revalidation after
  save.

## Product List

- Open **Catalog** and use the **Products** section.
- Search by product name or refresh the list when catalog data may be stale.
- Use **Manage Product Images** for shared product media.
- Use **Manage dynamic pricing presets** before assigning reusable dynamic
  pricing rules.
- Use **Add Product** to create a new product. Development builds may also show
  an import action.

## Product Actions

From the product row action menu you can:

- Edit or duplicate a product.
- Create a note linked to the product.
- Open product ratings.
- Link the product to a channel, customer, supplier, or warehouse.
- Delete the product after confirming it is no longer needed.

## Create a Product

Use this workflow for a new product that should be available in the active
channel:

1. Open **Catalog** -> **Products** and confirm the active channel.
2. Select **Add Product**.
3. Fill name, description, category, active state, and channel context.
4. Add SEO slug, title, and description before publishing to the storefront.
5. Choose the price type and complete the required price fields.
6. Select product type, attributes, page-count behavior, and size/order limits
   when the product needs configuration.
7. Add media and product-specific images.
8. Preview the storefront configuration and listing prices.
9. Save the product, then review translations and storefront visibility.

## Product Form

The product form covers:

- Name, description, category, active state, and channel.
- SEO slug, title, and description for storefront pages.
- Images and product-specific media.
- Price type, price tables, matrix or threshold pricing, and dynamic pricing.
- Attributes, product type, page-count pricing, expiration, and store listing
  price previews.

## Pricing Workflows

- Use a single price for simple fixed products.
- Use volume or threshold prices when the product has quantity breaks.
- Use matrix pricing when price depends on a combination of attributes.
- Use page-count pricing for products where the number of pages changes the
  price.
- Use dynamic pricing presets for reusable rules, then link those presets from
  product forms.
- Preview default, low, and high listing prices before saving products that
  appear in the storefront.

## Link Products to Channels

Use **Link to Channel** when the same product should be available in another
channel without maintaining a duplicate record.

1. Open the product row action menu.
2. Select **Link to Channel**.
3. Choose a target channel. The dialog only lists channels other than the active
   one.
4. Confirm with **Link**.
5. Refresh product search or open an order form in the target channel to verify
   the product is available.

The link updates the product's linked-channel list and refreshes the product
search index. It does not create a separate product document in the target
channel. Edit the source product when prices, SEO, media, or configuration need
to change.

## Link Products to Customers

Use **Link to Customer** for products that should be associated with a specific
account, such as negotiated items, private catalog products, or repeat-order
templates.

1. Open the product row action menu.
2. Select **Link to Customer**.
3. Search for and select the customer.
4. Confirm with **Link**.
5. In a new order for that customer, check the linked-products area and verify
   the product can be selected.

Customer links are stored on the customer record. They also change pricing
behavior: the customer's global discount is not automatically applied to linked
products unless the order item uses its own custom discount.

## Example Workflows

### Publish a Storefront Product

Create the product, add valid prices, assign category and SEO, upload media,
review translations, then verify it from the storefront or an order form in the
same channel.

### Reuse a Product in Another Channel

Create and test the product in the source channel, use **Link to Channel**, then
search for it in the target channel. Keep future product edits on the source
record.

### Prepare a Customer-Specific Product

Create the product with the agreed specification, link it to the customer, and
create a test order for that customer to confirm selection and discount behavior.

### Update a Priced Product

Check whether the product uses fixed, matrix, page-count, or dynamic pricing.
Update the relevant price table, preview listing prices, save, and verify order
pricing before operators use it for live quotes.

## Translations and Storefront Impact

- Product translations can be generated and reviewed from catalog translation
  controls.
- Slug changes affect storefront product URLs and metadata cache.
- Pricing, availability, shipping, and express settings can affect how products
  appear in storefront lists and checkout.
- Linked-channel products can affect search and order forms outside the source
  channel, so verify every channel where the product is exposed.

## Before Publishing

- Confirm the product is in the intended category and channel.
- Check at least one valid price exists for the selected price type.
- Preview product media and SEO values.
- Review dynamic pricing rules before enabling dynamic products.
- Check linked customers and channels before deleting or heavily changing a
  product used by other workflows.
